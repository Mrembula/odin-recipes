Mogodu and Samp

Mogodu is a South African food. Mogodu is a combination of chopped tripe and intestines serves as a stew often with samp or pap

Ingredients

  • 1 kg tripe coarse salt, for sprinkling and soaking
  • 4 litres of water salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 teaspoon of ghee or vegetable oil
  • 1 medium onion, roughly chopped
  • 1 large red chilli, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic, crushed
  • 2 teaspoon of garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 x 400 g can of chopped tomatoes
  • 1 cup amasi or plain yoghurt, for serving
  • 2 teaspoon of parsley, chopped, to garnish

Instructions

  1. Place the tripe in a large bowl
  2. Sprinkle coarse salt all over the tripe and add half the water
  3. Soak for about an hour, then rinse thoroughly under cold, running water
  4. Cut the tripe into bite-sized pieces and place in a large saucepan with a lid. Season with salt and pepper and add the remaining water
  5. Slowly bring it to a boil over a low heat and cook for 3 hours or until tender. Drain and set aside
  6. Heat the ghee or oil in a large pan or skillet
  7. Stir in the onion, chilli and garlic
  8. Add the masala, paprika and bay leaves. Season with salt and pepper, and stir through while cooking over low heat until the onion is cooker
  9. Add tomatoes, stir through, and cook for 2 minutes
  10. Add the amasi or yoghurt and stir through. The sauce should be thick by now, if not, cook over low heat until reduced
  11. Add the tripe and cook for about 10 minutes in the sauce
